I'm Ben; I live in San Francisco. I write software and do research, often aimed at improving or saving the world. I love thinking about artificial intelligence, philosophy, cryptography, and robotics.

As of mid-2025, I work as senior researcher at Palisade Research, trying to think clearly about risks from advanced AI agents, and part-time as acting director at AI Impacts.

In the past I've done research engineering work at the Machine Intelligence Research Institute and Redwood Research, built a novel cryptographic database for screening DNA syntehsis orders for biological hazards at SecureDNA, and helped build the on-car planning and prediction software for Cruise Automation's self-driving cars.

I'm originally from Blacksburg, Virginia, and lived there through my undergraduate degree, which was in Computer Engineering. In Blacksburg I spent an unusual amount of time in Bollo's Cafe and Bakery, and helped to start Hacksburg, Blacksburg's first community hackerspace.
